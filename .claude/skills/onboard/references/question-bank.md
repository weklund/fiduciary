# Question Bank

Organized by phase. Pick adaptively — never ask all of these. 1-2 per turn.

## Phase 1: Scope & Situation

### Opener (pick one)
- "What brought you to set this up today? Is there something specific on your mind, or are you looking for a general financial check-in?"
- "Are you here because something changed recently, or because you want to get more intentional about your money?"

### Household
- "Who's in your household financially? Just you, or is there a partner/spouse whose finances are intertwined?"
- "Anyone else depending on your income — kids, parents, siblings?"

### Employment
- "What does your income look like — single W-2, multiple streams, self-employed, something in transition?"
- "How stable does your income feel right now — locked in, or could it change in the next 6 months?"

### Life Stage
- "Roughly where are you in your career — early stage building skills, mid-career peak earning, winding down, or something else entirely?"
- "Any big life events on the horizon — job change, marriage, kids, relocation, retirement?"

---

## Phase 2: Vision & Values

### The Big Questions (pick 2-3 max)
- "What does 'financial freedom' look like for you specifically? Not the Instagram version — your actual version."
- "What's the one thing about your financial situation that, if it were solved, would let you sleep better?"
- "If money weren't a constraint for the next year, what would actually change about how you spend your days?"
- "When you think about money, what's the first emotion? Anxiety, control, freedom, guilt, excitement, indifference?"
- "Is there a financial decision from your past that still bothers you? What would you do differently?"
- "What does 'enough' mean to you — is there a number, a feeling, or a milestone?"

### Relationship with Debt
- "How do you feel about carrying debt — does it keep you up at night, or is it just a tool?"
- "Is being debt-free a core goal, or are you comfortable carrying low-interest debt indefinitely?"

### What Matters Most
- "If you had to pick one — maximum security, maximum freedom, or maximum growth — which wins?"
- "Would you rather have more money or more time? Where's that balance for you right now?"

---

## Phase 3: Hard Numbers

### If Plaid Connected (confirm/fill gaps)
- "I can see your accounts and recent transactions. Let me summarize what I see — correct me where I'm wrong."
- "Are there any accounts NOT connected here? Retirement, HSA, spouse's accounts, crypto?"
- "I can see your debt balances but not the interest rates. Can you tell me the APR on [specific debts]?"
- "What does your insurance look like — health, life, disability? Through an employer or private?"

### If Manual Collection
- "What's your rough gross household income — monthly or annual, whichever's easier?"
- "What are your major debts? For each: roughly how much, what's the interest rate, and what's the minimum payment?"
- "What about savings and investments — any retirement accounts, brokerage, emergency fund?"
- "What are your big fixed monthly costs — housing, car, insurance, subscriptions?"

### Tax Situation
- "How do you file — single, married filing jointly, head of household?"
- "Rough tax bracket? Or if easier: what's your total household W-2 income range?"
- "Any tax complications — self-employment income, stock options, rental property, crypto gains?"

---

## Phase 4: Goals & Time Horizons

### Time-Bucketed
- "In the next 12 months, what absolutely needs to happen financially? What's urgent?"
- "Looking out 1-5 years — what are you building toward? A milestone, a purchase, a transition?"
- "Long game — 5, 10, 20 years out — where do you want to be? What does that life look like?"

### Priority Ranking
- "If these goals conflict — if you can't do all of them — which one wins?"
- "Is there anything on this list that's non-negotiable? Something you'd sacrifice the others for?"

### Metrics
- "Is there a specific number or ratio you want to track? Something that would tell you you're winning?"
- "What would be a red flag for you — a number that, if you saw it, would mean something went wrong?"

---

## Phase 5: Risk & Behavioral Patterns

### Risk Tolerance (emotional)
- "Market drops 30% overnight. Be honest — do you hold, sell, or buy more?"
- "Have you ever panic-sold or panic-bought something financial? What triggered it?"
- "On a spectrum of 'check my portfolio daily' to 'haven't logged in this year' — where are you?"

### Risk Capacity (financial)
- "If all your income stopped tomorrow, how many months could you cover the essentials?"
- "Is your income concentrated (one job, one client) or diversified?"
- "Does anyone else depend on your income? Could you take a 50% pay cut for 6 months and be okay?"

### Behavioral Patterns
- "Do you want this system to automate decisions for you, or present options and let you decide?"
- "How often do you want to think about this — weekly check-ins, monthly, quarterly, only when something breaks?"
- "When unexpected money comes in — bonus, refund, windfall — what's your first instinct?"
- "Are you more likely to overspend on things (stuff) or experiences (dinners, travel, events)?"

### Stress Response
- "Under financial stress, do you tend to freeze, get hyper-active about cutting costs, or avoid looking?"
- "Is there a dollar amount threshold where a single expense stops feeling small? ($50? $200? $500?)"

---

## Phase 6: Wrap-Up

### Confirmation
- "Here's what I've captured about your situation. Does this feel accurate?"
- "Anything I'm missing or got wrong? Anything you'd want to add?"
- "Is there anything you want me to specifically NOT track or comment on?"

### Setting Expectations
- "How do you want to use this going forward — ask questions when something comes up, or get proactive weekly check-ins?"
- "Is there anything that should trigger an alert — a balance threshold, a spending spike, a missed payment?"
