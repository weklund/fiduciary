# Example Client Context Output

This is what a well-completed Client Context section looks like. Use as a reference
for tone, specificity, and structure — never copy directly.

---

## Client Context

- **Household:** Married, two kids (8 and 11), spouse works part-time
- **Income:** $145K base salary + $20K annual bonus (software engineer), spouse $35K/yr part-time nursing
- **Employment:** Stable W-2 at mid-size tech company, 4 years tenure, no immediate risk
- **Location:** Austin, TX (no state income tax, high cost of living)
- **Life stage:** Mid-career, peak earning years, college funding horizon 7-10 years

### Financial Position
- **Cash/Savings:** $28K across checking + HYSA
- **Investments:** $220K 401(k) + $45K Roth IRA + $12K taxable brokerage
- **Total debt (non-mortgage):** $18K auto loan at 4.9%, $3K credit card (paid monthly)
- **Mortgage(s):** $2,800/month ($380K remaining at 3.2% — locked in 2021)
- **Insurance:** Health through employer (family plan), $500K term life (20yr), no disability
- **Tax situation:** MFJ, ~24% marginal bracket, standard deduction, no complications

### Goals (Prioritized)
1. Max retirement savings across all vehicles (ongoing, annual)
2. Fund 529s for both kids — target $150K each by age 18 (7-10 year horizon)
3. Build taxable brokerage to $200K for optionality (5-7 years)
4. Pay off auto loan early if rate environment makes it sensible (12 months)

### Key Metrics to Track
| Metric | Current | Target | Red Flag |
|--------|---------|--------|----------|
| Savings rate (gross) | 18% | 25% | <15% |
| Emergency fund (months) | 3.2 | 6 | <2 |
| Debt-to-income | 22% | <20% | >36% |
| 529 funding pace | $200/mo/kid | $500/mo/kid | $0 |

### Behavioral Profile
- **Risk tolerance:** Moderate-aggressive for long-term, conservative for <3yr goals. Would hold through a 30% drop but would feel sick about it.
- **Money mindset:** Views money as security first, freedom second. Grew up in household where money was tight — has scarcity patterns even when objectively fine.
- **Decision style:** Prefers "set and forget" automation. Checks accounts weekly but doesn't trade or tinker. Wants quarterly reviews, not weekly.
- **Stress pattern:** Under financial stress, tendency is to freeze and avoid rather than panic-sell. Needs external prompts to take action.

### Constraints & Guardrails
- Never touch 401(k) early for any reason
- Keep 6 months cash before accelerating any debt payoff
- Auto loan payoff only if savings rate stays above 20%
- If either spouse loses job: freeze all non-essential spending immediately, extend emergency fund target to 9 months

### Review Cadence
- Monthly: quick budget check, confirm auto-investments running
- Quarterly: full review with metrics, rebalance if >5% drift
- Annually: tax planning, insurance review, goal progress check
- Event-triggered: any income change >10%, major expense >$5K, life event
